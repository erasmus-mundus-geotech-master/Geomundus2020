GeoMundus 2020

Geomundus Mapathon

This is a competition that allows participants to showcase their work with the world and share their brilliant ideas and findings. The competition is open worldwide (to registrants) and is aligned with the 12th edition of Geomundus conference which is to be held as a Digital Conference this year. The theme for this year’s Mapathon is : Mapping our future on Earth. Three categories can be visualised:

Topics:
  1. Covid-19
  2. Green (renewable) energy
  3. Climate Change and environment
    1. Risks and natural disasters (Flood mapping, Hurricane frequency, etc.)
    2. Human-induced changes on the environment (deforestation, pollution, etc.)

We believe these are relevant issues and that creative liberty can be taken to make both attractive and informative maps!

The Geomundus Mapathon competition is open to all students and recent graduates (at most in 2020) who have registered for Geomundus 2020. Share your research results or map an issue that you feel deserves more recognition, we are looking forward to each kind of submission. As cartographers, you have the freedom to visualise and portray any of the above issues on a map. Map your story of the COVID-19 experience, or share the efforts of your country in response to the corona outbreak, an interesting phenomenon resulting from our changing climate or a new visualization technique for renewable energy. Maps are useful to bring awareness to oft ignored and underrepresented issues like renewable energy transition and climate change.

The Challenge

Geomundus Mapathon intends to promote digital map production and the use of typical map elements. Submission will be in the form of a pdf document of your map product which should be of A3 size (landscape or portrait). Please see the Mapathon Submission Guidelines and Mapathon Evaluation Rubric for more information.

The challenge for you is to develop a map with all relevant map elements, illustrating the information you would like to highlight or bring awareness to. There are two groups: individual and group (maximum 3 members). The selected winners will receive prizes from a pool of 825 Euros (in the form of Paypal gift cards).

Who can participate?

Geomundus Mapathon is open for all students registered for Geomundus 2020 (currently enrolled, enrolling or graduated in 2020). Students from any university or high school can participate. Whether you are a student who started learning Cartography recently or an expert who creates maps routinely, a newcomer to GIS technology or a GIS professional; the challenge is for you.

Deadline for your submission via Geomundus website is November 01, 2020.

Sample Submissions

Although these maps are not under the mentioned themes, we have added them to give guidance as they are aesthetically pleasing and exemplify requisite elements that maps should have.

The above map was made given the prompt of showing the difference between normalized and raw population data. Classification methods can be elusive and the map design was encouraged to be Japan-centric. Some stereotypes of what this meant were extrapolated but of course the overall design was aesthetically pleasing and informative.

For the above map, students were encouraged to build a children’s map that divided a certain region based on some characteristics. The watercolour style available from ESRI by John Nelson was the foundation for these maps. There are many different styles and customizations that you can leverage, even outside of using ArcGIS. We encourage you to explore your options and let the creative juices flow!

The final sample visualized elevation data from Mexico overlaid with a futuristic space theme. Of course apart from the mapping, some graphic design tools and software were used to achieve this result. A very impressive map that combined a space theme with elevation data to draw in the audience and map some interesting features.
