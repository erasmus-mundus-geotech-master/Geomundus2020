GeoMundus 2020

MAPATHON

This is a competition that allows participants to showcase their recent work with the world to share their brilliant ideas and findings. The competition is open worldwide (to registrants) and is aligned with the 12th edition of Geomundus conference which is to be held as a Digital Conference this year. Mapathon is open to all domains of GIS, remote sensing and location based services and offers a great opportunity for participants to directly share their work with the GIS community, from professionals to aspiring GIS analysts.

The Geomundus Mapathon event is a competition for all as it's a conference for students by students. Share your research results or map an issue that you feel deserves more recognition, we are looking forward to each kind of submission. Map your story of the COVID-19 experience, or share the efforts of your country in response to the corona outbreak. Any interesting phenomenon you experienced or a new technology you tried. Maps are useful to bring awareness to oft ignored and underrepresented issues like human rights and climate change.

The Challenge

Geomundus Mapathon intends to promote digital map production and the use of typical map elements. Submission will be in the form of a pdf document of your map product which should be of A3 size (landscape or portrait).

The challenge for you is to develop a map with all relevant map elements, illustrating the information you would like to highlight or bring awareness to. The top three winners will receive prizes from a pool of close to 800 Euros (from Paypal eGiftcards). There are two groups: individual and group (maximum 3 members).

Who can participate?

Geomundus Mapathon is open for all students (currently enrolled or graduated at most in 2019). Students from any university can participate. Whether you are a student who started learning Cartography recently or an expert who creates maps routinely, a newcomer to GIS technology or a GIS professional for years; the challenge is for you.

Deadline for your submission via Geomundus website is November 01, 2020.

All submissions will be shared on the conference website. Winners will be announced during the conference.
